City Latitude & Humidity


Humidity is controversial. Most people hate it, yet some people love it. One thing that is not controversial about humidity is its lack of correlation to latitude according to this figure. All the data points together resemble a blob with no clear direction or shape, unlike the visualization of Latitude & Temperature. But is that the whole story; that there isn't any relationship here to be seen?

What could be at play here that is preventing a clear correlation from emerging? Well, there are many confounding variables that make it difficult to see a clear relationship. For example, time of year! We didn't control for the time of year, and the relationship at stake here might vary by the season. There's also the cities themselves. For this analysis, we randomly selected 500 cities. Were those cities evenly distributed geographically? Are they well known for having microclimates? And of course, we aren't controlling for prevailing weather either. For instance, you might have two cities on the same line of latitude. The city to the west might be having rainy day while the city to the east is enjoing blue skies. All these variables are at play and complicate the story!
